# SOCIETIES: Comprehensive Breakdown of Features and Gameplay
*An AI-Enhanced Multiplayer Civilization Simulation*

## 1. Overview and Objective
Societies is an open-world environmental simulation game that blends survival, crafting, economics, and governance in a multiplayer setting enhanced by AI-driven agents.
Players inhabit a small virtual planet (0.52km2 - 4km2), teeming with plants and animals, working alongside AI citizens to build a civilization advanced enough to face escalating existential threats—all while maintaining ecological balance in a persistent, evolving world.

The game begins with all participants—human and AI alike—as independent homesteaders, each with nothing but basic tools and a small land claim. From this primitive state, players must cooperate to form increasingly complex social structures: neighborhoods, towns, states, countries, and eventually planetary federations. This societal progression is not merely a backdrop—it is the core gameplay loop. The game is fundamentally about **building society itself**, with survival, crafting, and economics serving as the mechanisms through which social organization becomes necessary and meaningful.

### What Makes Societies Different
*   **Society as Core Gameplay**: The progression from homesteader to citizen of a planetary federation IS the game.
*   **AI-Human Equivalence**: AI agents are not NPCs or tools—they are citizens with the same rights, roles, and capabilities as human players.
*   **Persistent World**: The simulation continues whether humans are online or not.
*   **Escalating Challenges**: Threats progress beyond a single meteor, creating ongoing purpose.
*   **Emergent Politics**: Governance is not a minigame but a necessity.

## 2. Core Design Philosophy
### The Fundamental Principle: Equivalence
The foundational principle of Societies is that **AI agents and human players are the same type of entity**, differing only in their controller.
*   **Economic equivalence**: AI agents buy, sell, produce, and consume.
*   **Political equivalence**: AI agents can vote, hold office, and propose laws.
*   **Social equivalence**: AI agents have reputations and form relationships.
*   **Labor equivalence**: AI agents perform the same jobs as humans.

### Why Equivalence Matters
*   **The Continuity Problem**: When humans log off, AI continues production.
*   **The Demand Problem**: AI agents continuously consume goods, preventing economic stagnation.
*   **The Tedium Problem**: Repetitive labor can be performed by AI agents.
*   **The Political Mass Problem**: Laws govern a real population, giving weight to politics.
*   **The Server Death Problem**: AI provides resilience against player attrition.

## 3. World and Ecosystem Simulation
The game world is a discrete spherical planet with diverse biomes (Boreal Forest, Rainforest, Desert, etc.).
*   **Dynamic Ecosystem**: Predator-Prey dynamics, Plant Reproduction, Habitat Requirements.
*   **Environmental Dynamism**: Seasonal Cycles, Weather Events, Natural Disasters, Disease Outbreaks.
*   **Climate System**: Solar input, CO2 levels, Ocean currents, Ice-albedo feedback.

## 4. Environmental Impact and Pollution
*   **Ground Pollution**: Tailings, Garbage, Sewage, Chemical spills.
*   **Air Pollution**: Smog, CO2 Emissions, Industrial emissions.
*   **Management**: Containment, Treatment, Prevention, Remediation, Regulation.
*   **Collapse**: Species extinction, Ecosystem collapse, Agricultural failure, Climate catastrophe.

## 5. AI Population System
*   **Autonomy**: Agents pursue their own interests.
*   **Elasticity**: AI population scales based on human activity.
*   **Authenticity**: AI agents have genuine stakes in the simulation.
*   **Distribution**: System monitors Economic participation, Labor coverage, Geographic distribution.
*   **Characteristics**: Identity, Specialization, Preferences, Relationships, Bounded rationality.

## 6. Society and Governance
The progression from isolated homesteader to planetary federation.

### Phase 1: Homesteading
*   Basic survival, limited land claims, no formal laws.

### Phase 2: Neighborhood Formation
*   Informal cooperation, mutual aid, no enforcement.

### Phase 3: Town Formation
*   Formal constitution, enforceable laws, taxation, public services.

### Phase 4: Town Growth
*   Citizen recruitment, territorial expansion, trade agreements.

### Phase 5: State/Country Formation
*   Federal structure, interstate laws, large infrastructure, unified currency.

### Phase 6: Multi-State Relations
*   Alliances, trade treaties, resource conflicts.

### Phase 7: Federation (Planetary Government)
*   Global laws for existential threats, planetary projects.

## 7. Resource Gathering and Crafting
*   **Early Game**: Foraging, Hunting, Logging, Basic crafting.
*   **Mid Game**: Agriculture, Animal husbandry, Metallurgy, Masonry.
*   **Late Game**: Electronics, Power systems, Advanced materials, Laser technology.
*   **Consumption**: Tool wear, Building maintenance, Food spoilage.

## 8. Skills, Specialization, and Roles
*   **Categories**: Survivalist, Farmer, Hunter, Chef, Carpenter, Mason, Smith, Engineer, Tailor, Scientist.
*   **Progression**: Passive accumulation + Active bonuses.
*   **Specialization**: Mandatory interdependence.

## 9. Economy and Trade
*   **Currency**: Personal credit -> Minted currency -> Fiat currency.
*   **Stores**: Asynchronous buying and selling.
*   **Contracts**: Work contracts, Supply contracts.
*   **Perpetual Demand**: Driven by AI consumption and durability/spoilage.

## 10. Threats and Endgame Progression
*   **Phase 1: The Meteor (Days 1-30)**: Requires lasers, lasers require industry.
*   **Phase 2: Environmental Reckoning (Days 30-60)**: Cleaning up the mess from Phase 1.
*   **Phase 3: Resource Transition (Days 45-90)**: Dealing with depletion (Peak Oil/Ore).
*   **Phase 4: External Threats (Days 60-120)**: Asteroid fields, Solar flares.
*   **Phase 5: Pandemic/Biological (Days 90-150)**: Blights, Invasives.
*   **Phase 6: Climate Tipping Points (Days 120+)**: Ice sheet collapse, geoengineering.
*   **Phase 7: Space Expansion**: Orbital infrastructure, Lunar settlement.

## 11. Multiplayer and Social Dynamics
*   **Server Types**: Public, Private, Single-player.
*   **Temporal Flexibility**: World continues without you.
*   **Human-AI Social Dynamics**: AI as genuine participants.

## 12. Additional Features
*   Land Ownership, Power Systems, Transportation, Research.

## 13. Conclusion
Societies aims to be a genuine society simulator where human creativity and AI resilience combine to create persistent worlds worth inhabiting.
