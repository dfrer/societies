# Societies Concept Bible

Status: **Living — accepted baseline plus explicitly marked open questions**

## Canonical North Star

> "A deterministic civilization/ecology simulation where humans and AI citizens work, trade, negotiate, govern, and experience shared consequences."

This document develops that promise in experiential language. It does not claim that future systems are implemented.

## Experience Promise

Societies should make the player feel like a consequential participant in a living community rather than an omnipotent manager watching disposable agents.

Humans and AI citizens should share one material world. They should depend on resources, institutions, agreements, labor, ecology, and one another. Their choices should create visible benefits, tradeoffs, conflicts, and consequences that persist beyond a conversation.

## Accepted Product Pillars

### One authoritative world

The deterministic simulation owns facts, time, resources, ecology, eligibility, policy state, and world-changing outcomes. Every mutation enters through validated commands and events.

### Consequential human participation

The human must make readable choices that materially affect citizens and the world. AI participation enriches human agency; it does not automate the player out of the experience.

### AI citizens with material standing

AI citizens should eventually possess understandable needs, interests, responsibilities, preferences, and institutional roles. They are participants in the simulation, not omniscient narrators or privileged administrators.

### Shared consequences

Work, scarcity, trade, policy, negotiation, and ecological pressure should affect both humans and AI citizens through the same authoritative systems.

### Language as participation, not authority

Future LLM capabilities may interpret structured state, deliberate, communicate, negotiate, summarize bounded memory, and propose actions. Model output never directly changes authoritative state.

### Resilient simulation

Offline operation, invalid model output, provider failure, and timeout must preserve deterministic progress and replay through a visible fallback path.

## Intended Experience Layers

1. **Material layer:** land, resources, ecology, production, logistics, needs, and scarcity.
2. **Social layer:** citizens, roles, relationships, reputation, obligations, and conflict.
3. **Economic layer:** ownership, labor, exchange, prices, contracts, and institutions.
4. **Civic layer:** proposals, negotiation, collective decisions, governance, enforcement, and legitimacy.
5. **Language layer:** explanation, persuasion, testimony, negotiation, memory summaries, and narrative interpretation.

Each upper layer must remain grounded in the deterministic layers below it.

## Current Concept Boundary

The near-term product question remains:

> Can AI citizens hold understandable material interests and participate with a human in one consequential civic decision?

The current prototype should answer that bounded question before the project expands into general markets, general lawmaking, multiplayer, broad social simulation, or live LLM integration.

## Foundational Questions Still Open

The highest-priority unknowns are maintained in [OPEN-QUESTIONS.md](OPEN-QUESTIONS.md). The first interview must determine:

- who the human player is within the society;
- what formal and informal power the player possesses;
- why citizens cooperate with, tolerate, challenge, or resist the player;
- what the player risks losing; and
- what kind of shared consequence makes the society feel alive.

Do not silently answer these questions in this document. Record user-approved answers in [DECISIONS.md](DECISIONS.md) first.
